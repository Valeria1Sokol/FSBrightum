Start with revitalizing
skin epidermal cells and
fibroblasts

A clinical study with 20 volunteers aged from 35 to 65 was conducted to evaluate efficacy of Brightum* on human skin renewal. The pigmentation model (DHA) is used to quantify the skin renewal capacity. Pigmentation is eliminated faster if the cell renewal is activated. Cream containing pseudo substance or Brightum was applied twice a day for 12 days.

Brightum clinically improves
human skin renewal

The results indicate that Brightum* improved skin renewal, more than
doubled in just 4 days (p˂0.001).

Human dermal fibroblasts were treated with Brightum** as 0.15% and 1.5%. The same fibroblasts treated with vehicle only were served as negative control. The cell number was quantified for each treatment after 48-hours.

Brightum enhances skin fibroblast
proliferation

The results indicate that Brightum* improved skin renewal, more than doubled in just 4 days (p˂0.001).

Improve skin’s hydration
and barrier function

A clinical study was conducted with 20 volunteers aging from 35 to 65 to evaluate the efficacy of Brightum* in improving skin hydration. Cream containing pseudo substance or Brightum was applied twice a day for 28 days. At the beginning and end of the study, skin hydration was assessed by corneometer.

Brightum clinically improves
skin hydration

The results indicate that 95% of volunteers observed significant improvement. The average hydration improvement is as high as 16.5%. The 8.8% difference between Brightum* and the control indicates a well-improved skin barrier function. *p˂0.05, **p˂0.01,***p˂0.001

To evaluate the genetic impact of Brightum* on human keratinocytes, total RNA was extracted from human keratinocytes treated with/without Brightum. Quantitative real-time PCR (qRT-PCR) was performed to profile change of gene expression of various skin biology – related genes.

Build a healthier,
smoother and
softer skin

Microrelief is defined as cutaneous microstructure resembling irregular furrows on the skin surface. Microrelief pattern on skin has been widely used to evaluate skin health and aging. To evaluate the effect of Brightum* on skin microrelief, a clinical study of 20 volunteers aged from 35 to 65 was conducted. Cream containing with/without Brightum was applied twice a day for 28 days. At the beginning and end of this study, skin texture was evaluated by the profilometry.

Brightum clinically decrease
microrelief

The results indicate that Brightum* decreases the skin’s microrelief, -6.3% on average and up to -42.9%. These data suggest the role of Brightum in smoothing and softening the skin texture. **p˂0.01

Enhance skin clarity and luminosity by targeting pigment cells

Irregular accumulating of melanin within the epidermis can cause uneven tones and skin pigmentation. Melanin is produced by melanocyte, a pigment cell within epidermis. Within melanocytes, an enzyme called tyrosinase utilizes tyrosine to generate pigment melanin. To evaluate the effect of Brightum*** on melanin production, an in-vitro assay was generated to mimic the process which tyrosinase utilizes tyrosine to generate melanin, plus Brightum could be added. The results were obtained by quantifying melanin levels.

Brightum significantly reduce
melanin production

The results indicate that Brightum*** significantly reduces melanin production. These data also suggest the potential role of Brightum in mediating melanocyte to produce less pigment melanin.

To further evaluate the effect of Brightum*** on human skin tones, a clinical study of 24 volunteers were conducted. Cream containing with/without Brightum was applied twice a day for 28 days. At the beginning and end of this study, the color of skin was measured by a chromameter. The results were analyzed by using skin color topology. Data was summarized by using typology angles generated from volunteers.

Brightum enhances skin fibroblast
proliferation

The results indicate that Brightum*** significantly increases skin clarity and decreases skin pigmentation.

Reprogram dermal fibroblasts to enhance collagen production

Collagen loss can cause fine lines and wrinkles. The loss of collagen can also reduce skin elasticity. To evaluate the effect of Brightum** on collagen production, in-vitro studies were performed. Human dermal fibroblasts from healthy donners were cultured with/without Brightum for 48 hours. At the end of the study, collagen synthesis was evaluated.

Brightum stimulates Type I
collagen production

The results indicate that Brightum** enhances type I collagen synthesis of human dermal fibroblasts.

To further evaluate the effect of Brightum** in a clinical setting, 42 volunteers with wrinkles in the crow’s feet area were recruited to use cream with/without Brightum twice a day and total for 28 days. At the beginning and the end of the study, the depth of crow’s feet wrinkles was evaluated by skin profilometry.

Brightum clinically improves
winkle depth

The results indicate that Brightum** decreases the wrinkle depth of human skin, as high as 9.1% average in comparison with the control. **p˂0.01
